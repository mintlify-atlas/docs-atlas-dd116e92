## Overview

The Law Enforcement Officer (LEO) interface provides tools for officers to manage their on-duty activities, conduct searches, create records, and coordinate with dispatch and other units.

## Getting Started

### Creating Your Officer

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to My Officers">
    Go to **Officer** → **My Officers** from the main navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create Officer">
    Click **Create Officer** and fill in the details:
    - Select a citizen to base the officer on
    - Choose department and division
    - Select badge number
    - Upload officer image (optional)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Callsign">
    Your callsign will be generated based on your department's configuration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Going On-Duty

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Officer Dashboard">
    Navigate to the **Officer** page from the main navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Officer">
    Click **Select Officer** and choose which officer to go on-duty with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Initial Status">
    Choose your initial 10-code status (e.g., 10-8 for available).
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Dashboard">
    You're now on-duty and can see active calls, units, and warrants.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Only one officer can be on-duty at a time per user.
</Note>

## Changing Status

Update your status to communicate your availability and current activity:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Status Area">
    Your current status is displayed in the status area of the utility panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select New Status">
    Click on the status dropdown and select a new 10-code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Notes">
    Optionally add notes about your status change.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Common status codes:
- **10-8** - Available for calls
- **10-7** - Out of service
- **10-6** - Busy
- **10-97** - Arrived on scene
- **10-23** - Arrived at scene

<Tip>
  Your CAD administrator can configure custom 10-codes specific to your department.
</Tip>

## Search Functions

### Name Search

Search for citizens to view their records and information:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Name Search">
    Click **Name Search** in the utility panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Search Criteria">
    Search by:
    - First name and last name
    - Date of birth
    - Social Security Number (if enabled)
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Results">
    Select a citizen from the results to view:
    - Personal information
    - License status (driver's, pilot, weapon, etc.)
    - Active warrants
    - Criminal records
    - Registered vehicles
    - Registered weapons
  </Step>
</Steps>

From the search results, you can:
- Create arrest reports, tickets, or written warnings
- Create warrants
- View detailed records
- See flags or alerts on the citizen

### Vehicle Search (Plate Search)

Run a plate check on vehicles:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Plate Search">
    Click **Plate Search** in the utility panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Plate Number">
    Type the vehicle's license plate number or VIN.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Results">
    Review information including:
    - Owner name and details
    - Vehicle make, model, and color
    - Registration status
    - Insurance status
    - DMV status (if enabled)
    - Vehicle flags or alerts
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Weapon Search

Search for registered weapons:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Weapon Search">
    Click **Weapon Search** in the utility panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Serial Number">
    Input the weapon's serial number.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Registration">
    See the weapon's registration details and owner information.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Business Search

Look up business information:

- Business name and owner
- Business address
- License status
- Employees

## Traffic Stops and Records

### Creating a Traffic Stop Record

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search for Citizen">
    Use Name Search or Plate Search to find the person you've stopped.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Record Type">
    From the search results, select:
    - **Written Warning** - For minor infractions
    - **Ticket** - For citable offenses
    - **Arrest Report** - For arrests
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Violations">
    Choose violations from the penal code list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Details">
    Enter:
    - Notes about the stop
    - Location
    - Postal code (if required)
    - Officer narrative
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Penalties">
    For arrests:
    - Set jail time
    - Add fines
    - Determine if the person should be jailed immediately
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create Record">
    Click **Create** to save the record to the citizen's history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  All records are permanently stored and viewable by authorized personnel.
</Note>

## Warrant Management

### Creating a Warrant

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Warrant Modal">
    Click **Create Warrant** in the utility panel, or create from a name search result.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Citizen">
    Choose the citizen the warrant is for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Details">
    - **Description** - Reason for the warrant
    - **Status** - Active or Inactive
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create Warrant">
    The warrant will appear in the active warrants list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Viewing Active Warrants

The **Active Warrants** section on your dashboard shows all active warrants. Click on any warrant to:
- View full details
- Serve the warrant
- Update warrant status

## BOLO Management

### Creating a BOLO

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open BOLO Modal">
    Click **Create BOLO** in the utility panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Type">
    Select person, vehicle, or other.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Information">
    Provide detailed description of what to look out for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share with Units">
    The BOLO will be visible to all active units and dispatch.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Viewing Active BOLOs

Check the **Active BOLOs** section to see all current BOLOs. You can:
- View details
- Update BOLOs you created
- Remove expired BOLOs

## Working with 911 Calls

### Responding to Calls

<Steps>
  <Step title="View Active Calls">
    See all active 911 calls in the **Active Calls** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Self-Assign">
    Click **Assign Self** on a call to add yourself to it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update Status">
    As you respond:
    - Update your 10-code (e.g., 10-76 for en route)
    - Add notes to the call
    - Update call description as needed
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close Call">
    Once resolved, mark yourself as available or close the call.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Dispatch can also assign you to calls. You'll see the assignment in real-time.
</Tip>

## Incident Coordination

For major events, incidents help coordinate multiple units:

- View assigned incidents in the **Active Incidents** section
- Add events and updates to the incident
- Coordinate with other assigned units
- View incident timeline and all related calls

## Jail Management

### Viewing Jailed Citizens

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Jail">
    Go to **Officer** → **Jail** from the navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View List">
    See all citizens currently in jail with their:
    - Arrest information
    - Remaining time
    - Charges
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Releasing Citizens

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Citizen">
    Click on a jailed citizen from the list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Release Type">
    - **Released** - Time served or other reasons
    - **Bail Posted** - Citizen posted bail
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Release">
    The citizen will be removed from the jail roster.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Impound Lot

Manage impounded vehicles:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Impound Lot">
    Go to **Officer** → **Impound Lot**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View Impounded Vehicles">
    See all vehicles currently impounded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Release Vehicle">
    Select a vehicle and click **Release** when authorized.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Notepad

Use the notepad to keep personal notes during your shift:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Notepad">
    Click **Notepad** in the utility panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take Notes">
    Write notes about ongoing situations, citizen information, or anything else.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Notes are automatically saved and persist across sessions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Panic Button

<Warning>
  Use the panic button only in genuine emergency situations where you need immediate assistance.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Activate Panic">
    Click the **Panic Button** in the utility panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dispatch Notified">
    Dispatch and all units receive an immediate alert.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for Response">
    Backup units will be dispatched to your location.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Always Update Your Status">
    Keep your 10-code status current so dispatch knows your availability and location.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Document Thoroughly">
    Provide detailed notes in all records. Include relevant facts, circumstances, and observations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Verify Information">
    Always run name and plate searches before taking action to ensure you have accurate information.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Coordinate with Dispatch">
    Communicate with dispatch when responding to calls, changing locations, or encountering issues.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Check for Warrants">
    Always check for active warrants during traffic stops and citizen interactions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
